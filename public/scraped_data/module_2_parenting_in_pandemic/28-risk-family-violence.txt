
H2 Increased risk of family violence during the pandemic
Why is the risk of violence increased?
There is no single cause of family violence. It is a complex problem that can affect anyone at any time. However, some of the risk factors for family violence are increased during the pandemic. For example, financial and emotional stressors are associated with risk of violence in the home. We know from previous disasters (e.g. natural disasters) that rates of family violence increase following stressful events.1,2
During the pandemic, the local restrictions, including lockdowns and school closures, mean that people are spending more time at home together and have reduced access to outside supports.
This may lead to more conflict, which can escalate into violence for some families. It can also make it harder for people to seek help when they need it.
This means that some young people will be at increased risk of violence during the pandemic — this includes abuse, neglect, intimate partner violence, and sexual violence.
Remember, violence is never okay, and it’s important to seek help if you or your teen are at risk.
What should I do if my teen or myself are at risk?
Remember, even during a lockdown, you can
always
leave your home to escape harm or the risk of harm.
If you or someone else is in immediate danger, call 000.
