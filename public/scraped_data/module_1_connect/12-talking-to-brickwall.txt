
H1 It's like talking to a brick wall!
If your teenager doesn't respond to your attempt to communicate, try stepping back and looking at how you communicate.
H2 Do
Respectyour teenager's opinion, even if you disagree.“I haven't thought of it that way.”
Listen- teenagers often talk more when parents talk less.
Tune in to your teenager. They might be communicating with you in their own way. A slamming door could be your teenager's way of telling you they've had a rough day at school.
Consider your reactions. You may find your teenager's interests strange or dull and show your dislike on your face. Instead,stay interested and engaged.
H3 Don't
Make critical comments. Rather than blame your teenager for not communicating with you, express your desire to connect with them. Instead of"You never talk to me anymore!", try"I'd love to spend some time with you. What would you like to do together?"
Nag at your teenager. If the only thing your teenager hears from you is what they should be doing, they are less likely to talk to you.
Try to fix everything. Sometimes teenagers don't want advice, they just want to be listened to. Let your teenager share their world without swooping in to save them.
What's the best way your Mum or Dad could talk to you? What would you like from them when you are talking together?
