
H1 Identify, validate & understand
H4 Identify
Identifying and labelling the emotion helps your teenager feel heardand it helps you understand their world better. If you see that your teenager is feeling a strong emotion, ask about their feelings, e.g. "You look worried" or "It sounds like you're really angry".
Suggestions for labelling Emotions
H4 Validate
Acknowledge and show empathy for what your teenager is going through.Teenagers often feel like no one understands them or has ever experienced what they are going through. Hearing "I've been there, done that" can often leave a teenager feeling dismissed. Validating the feelings your teenager is telling you in conversation will help them feel understood. Try "That must be really tough" or "What a tricky situation." This helps to show empathy because it communicates your concern for their feelings.
H4 Understand
Explore what has led to the emotions.Ask your teenager about what has happened to make them feel this way. You could gently ask: "Is there something on your mind?", "What's making you so upset?" or "Would you like to talk about what's just happened?"
Letting your teenager tell you what's going on helps them understand what they are feeling and to manage their emotions effectively. Giving your teenager time to talk will be important.
