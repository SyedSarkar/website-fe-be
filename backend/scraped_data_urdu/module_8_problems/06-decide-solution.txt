
H1 Effective problem-solving

H3 4. Decide on a solution.
Choose a solution or a few solutions that your teenager thinks will work best.
You now need to plan exactly how it will work.
- Who will do what?
- When will they do it?
- What’s needed to put the solution into action?
[IMAGE: Mod7-p5.svg - encourage]
Next, write out a timeline of the action planto help you and your teenager see when to review the solution.
For example, the plan of action towards improving Maths grades includes doing Maths exercises every night as homework. It will help if your plan outlineswhat timethis will happen,whereit will take place andwhichexercises will be done. The plan could be reviewed after a week to see how the study routine is going, and then in a month’s time to see whether grades have improved.
Action steps for improving Maths grades | When | Done
Get extra Maths exercises from Mr. Smith and use these each evening. | Week 1
Find online study guides and use these each evening. | Week 2
Go to the library and borrow last year's exam, use as a practice exam for next week. | Week 3
Goal: Improve on my first term exam mark by 15%. | Week 4
Your teenager might need some coaching to feel confident with their solution. For example, if they are going to try to resolve a fight with a friend, they might find it helpful to practise with you what they are going to say.
