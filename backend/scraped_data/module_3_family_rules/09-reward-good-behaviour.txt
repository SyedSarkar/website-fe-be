
H1 Catch 'em being good
We all like to have our efforts and hard work acknowledged. When your teenager is behaving well, give them somepositiveconsequences. Use a variety of these, including praise, attention and privileges.
Notice when your teenager is following the rules and praise andthank them for it. Tell them you appreciate it, and try to do something special for them. This strategy often works best if you ‘catch’ your teenager behaving well. It’s always nice to hear a “well done” or “that’s great!” when you’re not expecting it.

H3 Here are a few things that teenagers like to hear from their parents:
- “Wow, your room looks so tidy. Nice job!”
- “Thanks for getting your homework done first.”
- A text message saying “Thanks for getting out of bed this morning without my help. It made my morning!”
