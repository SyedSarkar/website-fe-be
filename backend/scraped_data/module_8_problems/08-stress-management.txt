
H1 Stress management skills
Problems and obstacles in life can sometimes feel overwhelming and stressful. Here are some stress management strategies you can share with your teenager and use to manage your own stress too. By using these strategies yourself, you show your teenager how to cope well with stressful situations.
Focus on what you can control(your actions and reactions)and let go of what you cannot control(other people’s opinions and actions).
Break tasks down into small stepsso they are less overwhelming.
Don't try to be perfect and responsible for everything.If you need help with something, just ask for it!
Avoid overscheduling.Consider cutting out an activity or two, keeping only the most important ones.
Talkwith someone you trust about what you're going through and how it's affecting you.
Use relaxation techniques.Learn simple breathing exercises to help you relax. Try using them when you’re caught up in stressful situations.

Sleep well.Getting enough sleep is essential when dealing with stress. See the moduleGood health habits for good mental healthfor tips on improving sleep.
Exercise regularly.Regular exercise is a great way to manage stress. See the moduleGood health habits for good mental healthfor more information.
Eat well.A nutritious, balanced diet is important to keep you physically and mentally well. See the moduleGood health habits for good mental healthfor some tips on healthy eating.
Enjoy relaxing activities.Read a book, take a bath, spend time with your pet, listen to music, speak to a friend, go for a walk, or make time for a hobby.
Source: Center for Adolescent Health. Johns Hopkins Bloomberg School of Public Health
