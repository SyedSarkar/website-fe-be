
H1 Encourage a healthy diet

H3 What should my teenager eat?
Teenagers should eat a wide variety of nutritious foods from the five food groups: (1) Fruits and vegetables, (2) Potatoes, bread, rice, pasta and other starchy carbohydrates, (3) Beans, pulses, fish, eggs, meat and other proteins, (4) Dairy and alternatives, and (5) Oils and spreads.Click hereto find out more (you can alsoclick hereto download a copy of this chart).

H3 How much should my teenager eat?
This varies from person to person and depends on their age.Click here for more information.

H3 What foods should my teenager limit?
Limit foods containing:
- Added salt.
- Added sugar, e.g. lollies, soft drinks, cordial, fruit drinks and sports drinks.
- Saturated fats, e.g. cakes, biscuits, pastries, pizza, fried foods, processed meats, burgers and chips.
Research1,2,3shows that:
Eating a lot of processed foods (e.g. chips, soft drinks, hamburgers) can negatively affect mood.
26.6% of young Australians are overweight or obese. This can leave the young person feeling bad about themselves and increase their risk of depression and clinical anxiety.
