
H1 How?
Set aside some time to talkwith your teenager about your expectations for their behaviour.
With your teenager, decide on realisticconsequencesfor when rules are broken. These consequences should be unpleasant enough to be a deterrent, but not so harsh that they may damage your relationship with your teenager.
Suggestions of consequences
Make sure your teenager clearlyunderstandswhat the rules are and thereasonsbehind them.
It is useful towrite down a set of rules. Writing them down makes them clear, and can also prevent arguments about what is or isn’t allowed. For rules that are specific to your teenager, it may be a good idea to keep the rules somewhere a little private that’s still close at hand for when you need to refer to them, instead of making the rules public, such as by sticking them on the fridge.
