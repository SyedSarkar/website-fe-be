
H1 What family rules do we make?
Each family will have different family rules. The most important thing is to keep themsimpleandfew. This means being selective about what you make rules about. Here are some areas where you might want to set rules (click text below to reveal):
Safety
This can include the use of internet/electronic devices, curfews, alcohol use, sex, dating, etc. Here are some examples:
- A specific time that your teenager has to get home by after spending time with friends (curfew).
- Being allowed to go to parties that are adult-supervised, and where the host is a good friend of your teenager.
Daily routines/household chores
This includes homework, laundry, tidying their bedroom, eating at the dinner table, etc. Some examples include:
- Household chores that are your teenager’s responsibility to do each week.
- Being home for dinner on school nights.
- Keeping their room clean.
- Homework being completed each night.
- TV and electronic devices being turned off by a certain time each night.
How you treat each other
This includes manners, swearing, hitting, fighting, etc. Here are some specific examples:
- No swearing.
- Respect others and their things.
- No hurting others.
