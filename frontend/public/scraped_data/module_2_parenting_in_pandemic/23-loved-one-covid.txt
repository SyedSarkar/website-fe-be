
H2 If a loved one contracts COVID-19
None of us have lived through a global pandemic before. Even if your teen or family have dealt with illnesses in the past, COVID-19 brings with it different challenges — for example not being able to visit a person when they are sick, uncertainty about the long-term effects of the virus, and constant media coverage.
Your teen may respond to the news of a loved one contracting COVID-19 in many different ways. They might be worried, angry, or find it hard to believe. They could be optimistic or pessimistic about the situation. All of these reactions are normal and not necessarily reason for concern. If your teen is struggling to cope with how they’re feeling, think about how you could support them.
